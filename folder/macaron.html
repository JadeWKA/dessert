Macarons


Ingredients:

Macaron Shells:

Egg Whites (100 g)/
Sugar (90 g)
Almond Flour (140 g)
10x Sugar/ Confectionar Sugar/ Icing sugar (130 g)

American Butter Cream:

Soft Unsalted Butter ( 250 g )
Powdered Sugar/ Icing Sugar ( 500 g )
Vanilla Extract ( 1 Tbsp )
Milk ( 3-5 tbsp )


Process

1. Macaron Shells:

Place your egg whites in a bowl
Sieve the Icing sugar and whisk together in a separate bowl. Then whiz dry the ingredients in a food processor. This is to ensure that you have smooth shells and no bumpy ones. Hence, the more you do it, the better the result.

In your bowl of egg whites, use a wisk and set your machine on high. Once it gets frothy, you add the sugar little by little. This will take some time so do not rush this process or else your meringue will break and it could lead to hollow shells or break the shells.

Once that is ready, you add 1/3 of your ddry ingredients (almond flour and icing sugar) into the meringue. And then FOLD it (also known as macronage). Add another 1/3 and repeat this process until you have no more dry ingredients left to fold. Then you just PRESS your meringue, to get rid of air bubbles. To know when it is ready, you can form an 8 with your spatula. DO NOT OVER MIX THIS. If you do, you will see that it is too runny.

Now it is time for piping. Be sure when you pipe, you pipe on top. Do no tilt your hand and pipe sideways. Your macaron shells will not grow nicely. You can pipe this with parchment or silicon mats. When your aiming for a specific size, be sure to pipe a few cm less. This is because on the next step, you will take the whole tray and tap it down and they will increase its size. At the same time, it removes the air bubbles that still remain in the macarons. you don't want to tap it too much. If there are still some bumpy spots on your shells, you can use a skewer or a pin. Let it rest. Depending on your environment, you might have to wait 30 min - 1hr for this to be ready to bake in the oven. To know when they're ready, you will notice that your shells have become less shiny and they're dry to the touch. So be sure to gently touch it and see if they are ready to go.

Place your tray into the center rack of your oven at 300 degrees fahrenheit for 14 min. Be sure at 7 min, to flip them over so that the other half also gets baked well. It really depends on your oven, so if you see that they still haven't risen up, leave it for a few more minutes. Once they are ready, take them out and let it cool. If you immediately take it out, your bottom of the shell will stick to the parchment paper and ruin it.

American Buttercream: Soft butter meaning that you leave your butter out and it's soft to the touch. Wisk the butter into your machine for 5 min. Pour half of the icing sugar into the bowl and whisk it on the low or else the powdered sugar will blow all over the place. So start slowly and then increase the speed for 3 min. Then add the rest of the powdered sugar with vanilla extract and milk. Wisk it for 2 min until it is nice and fluffy. At this point you can add Food coloring and flavor and it is ready to be piped for your macarons. Done!
